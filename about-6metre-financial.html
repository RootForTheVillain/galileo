The Art

Take the time

Having spent my entire working career in financial services, I've learned a lot about customer service, relatability, and helping people understand and navigate a very complex industry. I've found that strategic financial decisions are often very emotional decisions for people to make. Clients want to understand the strategic benefit of a proposed solution and be 100% comfortable with their decision so that they are not left with the feeling of buyer's remorse. Taking the time to thoroughly explain concepts, openly discuss pros and cons, be transparent with product costs/fees, and answer questions are the key elements that build trust to create longstanding advisory relationships.

Do what you say you are going to do

Financial advisors are hired and fired based upon service. Clients want—and deserve—a financial professional that they can meet with consistently, returns calls promptly, and is accessible.

Stay the course

Let's face it—the markets will go up and the markets will go down. Investors, as a group, tend to diminish (or destroy) portfolio returns by acting on their emotions during difficult market cycles. Having a dedicated financial coach, who can help you deal with volatility and remind you of your long term financial goals can go a long way in helping you maintain perspective.

Specialization

The focal point of my practice is rooted in retirement income planning. People are taught throughout their career to save, save, save. When day one of retirement begins, how do you know how much you can spend so that you don't run out of money?

There are many articles and studies that address the idea of the 'safe withdrawal rate' in retirement. I have dedicated an enormous amount of time, attention, and research to this issue and have developed strategies to help clients achieve a successful retirement.
